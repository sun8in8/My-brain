## Reflective Wisdom
*Books that evoke a similar feeling to Tuesdays with Morrie — mortality, meaning, and how to live well.*

| Title | Author | Year | Core Theme | Notes |
|-------|--------|------|------------|-------|
| **Man's Search for Meaning** | Viktor E. Frankl | 1946 | Finding purpose amid suffering | Psychiatrist and Holocaust survivor. Introduces logotherapy — meaning, not pleasure, is our primary drive. Short and essential. |
| **The Last Lecture** | Randy Pausch | 2008 | Living fully when time is short | Carnegie Mellon professor delivers his final lecture after a terminal cancer diagnosis. Practical, warm, and surprisingly uplifting. |
| **The Prophet** | Kahlil Gibran | 1923 | Poetic philosophy on love, work, joy, and loss | A prophet speaks to a crowd before departing. Not a narrative — lyrical essays. Best read slowly, in pieces. |
| **When Breath Becomes Air** | Paul Kalanithi | 2016 | Identity, mortality, and what makes life meaningful | Neurosurgeon diagnosed with terminal lung cancer at 36. Beautifully written. Asks: what makes a life worth living? |
| **Meditations** | Marcus Aurelius | ~170 AD | Stoic philosophy — living well and accepting limits | Roman Emperor's private journal, never meant to be published. Practical and grounding. Recommended translation: Gregory Hays. |
