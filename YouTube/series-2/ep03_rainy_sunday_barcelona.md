# A Rainy Sunday in Barcelona 🌧️

## Script

### Opening — arrive into the rain (0:00–2:30)

*[Direction: Begin with rain. Not heavy — steady, soft, the kind that has been falling all morning and has no intention of stopping. 20–30 seconds before voice. Occasional distant bell.]*

**[Whisper]**
It's raining in Barcelona today.

*(a soft pause)*

It has been raining since before I woke up. I could hear it before I opened my eyes — that particular sound a Sunday makes when the whole city has decided, quietly and collectively, to stay inside.

*[Direction: Sound of a ceramic cup being lifted. Small sip. Cup back on saucer.]*

**[Whisper]**
I made coffee — real coffee, the kind that takes a few minutes and is worth it — and I carried it to the window. And I just stood there for a while. Watching the rain move across the glass.

*(gently)*

There's a word in Portuguese — *saudade* — for a kind of longing that isn't quite sadness. The Catalans have their own version of this feeling. They call it *morrinya*. A homesickness for somewhere you love, even when you're in it.

I think rainy Sundays do that to a place. They make you love it more quietly.

---

### Act one — the city at rest (2:30–9:00)

**[Soft-spoken]**
The street outside is almost empty. There's a man walking a small dog — neither of them seems particularly bothered by the rain. The dog is sniffing at something important near the base of a lamp post. The man is looking at his phone. In a moment they'll disappear around the corner and the street will be mine again.

*(pause)*

I've been coming to Barcelona for years. But I think I love it most like this — when the tourists have retreated indoors and the city exhales. When it remembers it's a place where people actually live.

*[Direction: Distant church bells — three strikes. A pause. Rain continues.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
When I first came here I tried to see everything. The Sagrada Família, the Picasso Museum, Park Güell before nine in the morning. I ticked things off a list.

*(a soft laugh)*

Now when I come, I make a different kind of list. The bakery on Carrer del Parlament that has the best croissant I've ever had anywhere. The bookshop near the Raval that organises its shelves by mood rather than genre. A particular bench in the Ciutadella park that faces the water.

*(gently)*

I've learned that the thing you remember from a place is almost never the thing you planned to see. It's the unplanned hour. The wrong turn that became the right one.

*[Direction: Soft turning of pages — as if looking through a notebook. Unhurried.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
I have a notebook I keep just for travel. Not an itinerary — more like a collection of small observations. Things I noticed. Textures. Overheard sentences I didn't quite understand. The colour of the light at a specific time of day.

I'm looking through it now.

*(pause)*

Here. I wrote this three years ago on a morning very much like this one:

*The old woman on the second floor across the street has been watering her geraniums in the rain. She is doing it deliberately. She seems pleased with herself.*

*(warmly)*

I don't know why I wrote that down. But every time I read it I feel something lift.

---

### Act two — inside, while it rains (9:00–17:00)

*[Direction: The interior settles. Pen on paper — slow, unhurried writing. ~40 seconds before voice.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
There's something that happens to time on a rainy Sunday in a city that isn't yours.

The normal pressures don't apply. You can't really justify sightseeing. You have a built-in reason to do nothing productive. The rain gives you permission.

*(pause)*

I think that's what I love about travel at its best — not the monuments, not the photos — but the temporary suspension of your ordinary obligations. For a few days you are allowed to be curious without agenda. Slow without guilt.

*(gently)*

I wonder sometimes why that feels so rare. Why we have to go somewhere else to give ourselves that permission. Why a rainy Sunday at home doesn't quite do the same thing.

*[Direction: Another sip of coffee. Rain intensifies slightly, then softens again.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
Maybe it's because at home there is always something you should be doing.

Here, the city doesn't know what you should be doing. The city just keeps raining and the geraniums keep getting watered and the man with the dog has long since gone home, and none of it requires anything from you.

*(very softly)*

I have been sitting at this window for an hour and a half. I have drunk two coffees. I have written three sentences in my notebook and crossed out two of them.

*(a small pause)*

It has been a perfect morning.

*[Direction: Slow page turning — long sequence, almost meditative. ~90 seconds. The rain is the main sound.]*

**[Whisper]**
I want to tell you about the market.

On Saturday mornings — when it isn't raining — there's a market near the Boqueria that sells things that aren't quite food and aren't quite art. Old maps of cities that no longer look like themselves. Hand-dyed fabric in colours that don't have names. Small ceramic bowls that fit perfectly in one hand.

I bought a bowl there once. Deep blue, slightly uneven, as if the person making it had been thinking about something else. I still have it. I drink tea from it on Sunday mornings when I want to feel somewhere else.

*(quietly)*

Objects carry places in them. That's why we bring things home. Not as proof that we were there — but as a way of keeping the feeling accessible.

A small blue bowl that says: *there is a rainy Sunday in Barcelona inside you, and you can go there whenever you need to.*

---

### Wind-down — the afternoon arrives (17:00–22:00)

*[Direction: The quality of light shifts — warmer. Rain beginning to ease. Soft, slow ambient fade.]*

**[Whisper]**
The rain is slowing down.

*(gently)*

In a little while it will stop altogether, and the streets will fill up again, and someone will open a window and someone else will call out to someone below, and the city will remember it has things to do.

But right now it's still raining.

*(pause)*

And you're here. Wherever here is for you tonight — your room, your sofa, your own version of a window with rain on it.

You brought yourself somewhere quiet. That's the same thing, really. The geography doesn't matter as much as the decision to stop for a moment. To let the day settle.

*[Direction: Very soft footsteps on wet cobblestone — a memory. Fades.]*

**[Whisper]**
Wherever you are tonight — I hope it feels a little like a rainy Sunday somewhere beautiful.

I hope you let yourself stay there a while.

*(very softly)*

Goodnight.

*[Direction: Rain fades slowly over 2–3 minutes to silence.]*
