## 4. Episode 05 — The Librarian

**Format:** Soft-spoken roleplay
**Length:** 20–25 minutes
**Tone:** Quietly uplifted
**Triggers:** Page turning, pen on paper, gentle typing, wooden tapping, door creak, rain ambience, clock sounds

---

### Script

#### Opening — settle in (0:00–2:00)

*[Direction: Begin in silence for 5–8 seconds. Ambient sounds: faint clock ticking, distant rain, occasional soft creak. ~30 seconds before voice.]*

**[Whisper]**
Oh — hello. I didn't hear you come in.

*(small soft smile in the voice)*

We're almost at closing time, but that's alright. You have until the hour. Come in, come in — please, don't rush.

*[Direction: Light tapping on a wooden desk as you gesture them over. Unhurried.]*

**[Whisper]**
This is my favourite time of day, actually. Just before close. Everything settles. The light goes a certain colour and the books get very quiet.

*(a soft pause)*

I know that sounds strange — books being quiet. But I think you might know what I mean, or you wouldn't be here at this hour.

---

#### Act one — finding what you need (2:00–8:00)

**[Soft-spoken]**
Now. What is it you're looking for today?

*(pause — as if listening)*

Mm. You're not entirely sure. That's actually the most honest answer anyone gives me, and also the most useful. It means we can really look.

*[Direction: Slow, deliberate page-turning through a large catalogue. ~45 seconds.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
Let me ask you something, and don't overthink it — *(soft pause)* — when you pick up a book and it feels right before you've read a single word, what does that feel like for you?

Is it the weight of it? The cover? Something about the title that just… lands?

*(gently)* I ask because I've been doing this a long time, and I've learned that what someone is really looking for in a book isn't always a story. Sometimes it's a feeling they've been missing. Sometimes it's permission for something.

*[Direction: Soft footsteps on wooden floor, fingers trailing along book spines. ~60 seconds.]*

**[Whisper]**
This section here — *(quiet tap on a shelf)* — this is where I put the books that are hard to categorise. They're not quite fiction, not quite not. They don't fit neatly anywhere, which I think is actually a mark in their favour.

The books that are difficult to shelve are usually the ones that change something.

*[Direction: Pull a book from the shelf. Blow a little dust from the cover. Open carefully — slow creak of the spine. Turn a few pages.]*

**[Whisper]**
Here. Hold this one for a moment.

*(pause)*

Don't read it yet. Just hold it. Sometimes that's enough to know.

---

#### Act two — the librarian's story (8:00–17:00)

*[Direction: This is the heart of the episode. Settle at the desk. Gentle typing then pen on paper. ~30 seconds before voice returns.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
I'll tell you something while you wait.

There was a woman who used to come here every Thursday evening. For about four years. Always at the same time — just before close, like you — and she always brought back exactly one book and took exactly one book.

She never asked for recommendations. She always knew what she wanted. I used to wonder how.

*(soft pause)*

One evening I finally asked her. I said — how do you always know? And she looked at me like it was the strangest question she'd ever heard. She said: *I just ask myself what I need to hear, and then I trust my hands to find it.*

*(quietly)*

I've thought about that almost every day since.

*[Direction: Slow page turn. A sip of tea — quiet cup on saucer. Rain softens.]*

**[Soft-spoken]**
I think a lot of people don't trust themselves that way anymore. There's so much noise telling you what you should want. What you should read. What should matter to you.

*(gently)*

But you came here tonight. To a quiet library, just before closing. That already tells me something about you.

It tells me you know, somewhere, that the thing you're looking for isn't loud.

*(very softly)*

The things worth finding rarely are.

*[Direction: Long, slow page-turning sequence — almost meditative. ~90 seconds minimum. Deeply unhurried.]*

**[Whisper]**
You know what I love most about this place?

Every single book in here was written by someone who sat alone with a blank page and decided — despite everything — that they had something worth saying.

*(pause)*

Every one of them was uncertain. Every one of them had days when they couldn't find the words. And yet — *(soft tap on the shelf)* — here they all are.

Still speaking. Still finding the right person at the right moment.

*(gently)*

Like tonight.

---

#### Wind-down — closing time (17:00–22:00)

*[Direction: Soft tidying sounds — books slid into place, drawer closing, lamp to warmer setting. Rain almost stopped. ~45 seconds.]*

**[Whisper]**
We're almost at the hour.

*(warmly, unhurried)*

Take the book. No — really. There's no rush to return it. Read it when it feels right, not when you feel like you should.

*(soft pause)*

And if it isn't quite what you needed — that's alright too. Sometimes a book's real job is just to be in your hands for a while. To remind you that you're someone who reads. Someone who looks for things.

*[Direction: Soft footsteps toward door. Creak of heavy wooden door held open. Faint cool air.]*

**[Whisper]**
Goodnight.

*(a beat — genuinely warm)*

Come back whenever you need to be somewhere quiet.

*[Direction: Door closes softly. Ambient returns — faint clock, last of the rain. Run 2–3 minutes before fading to silence.]*
